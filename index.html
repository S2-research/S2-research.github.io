Research

Research Evolution: From AI to Extended Reality

My research journey began in AI and behavioral science, where I contributed to understanding human-AI interaction patterns. However, I realized that the future of meaningful human-computer interaction lies in immersive experiences that blur the boundary between digital and physical worlds.

This insight led me to pivot toward Extended Reality (XR), where I now focus on developing intuitive, accessible immersive systems. My current work explores how XR can enhance human capabilities while maintaining natural interaction paradigms.

Current Research Focus: Developing adaptive XR interfaces that respond to user behavior and context, with applications in education, training, and collaborative work environments.

Leadership & Impact

I currently lead the LUT research contribution to a €4.9M multi-company research program funded by Business Finland. Our €500K portion focuses on developing next-generation immersive technologies for industrial applications.

This role has taught me to coordinate complex research initiatives, manage stakeholder expectations, and translate academic research into practical solutions that industry partners can implement.
