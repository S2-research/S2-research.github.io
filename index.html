About Me 🌸

I am a Junior Research Assistant in the AI and HCI Behavioural Lab at LUT University, Department of Software Engineering. I am also serving as Head Researcher & Project Worker for the MAISA project funded by Business Finland.

My research focuses on Human-Computer Interaction, Extended Reality, Social Computing, and Ethics in AI. I am particularly interested in developing Unity-based XR applications with physiological sensors to measure cognitive load and trust metrics in virtual environments.

I am currently pursuing my Master of Science in Software Engineering with a specialization in Digital Systems and Service Development at LUT University (2025-2027). I completed my Bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering with a minor in Practical Engineering, achieving distinction.

I am a core member of the Programming, Education, and Computer-Human Interaction Lab (PEACH Lab) and associated with the ETH AI Center and Swiss CHI communities.

Research Summary 🌸

My research explores the intersection of human-computer interaction and artificial intelligence, with a particular focus on trust, ethics, and user experience in AI systems. I investigate how people interact with AI technologies and how we can design more intuitive and trustworthy AI systems.

Key areas of my research include:

  • Trust in AI systems: Developing frameworks to measure and improve trust between humans and AI
  • Extended Reality (XR) applications: Creating immersive experiences with physiological monitoring
  • Human-AI collaboration: Designing hybrid intelligence systems for software development
  • AI ethics: Exploring ethical implications of AI in workplace and educational settings

My work combines quantitative methods (behavioral analytics, physiological measurements) with qualitative approaches (interviews, ethnographic studies) to provide comprehensive insights into human-AI interaction.
