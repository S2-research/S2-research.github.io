About

I'm a human-centered AI researcher focused on understanding how people interact with and trust AI systems across different contexts. My work spans Human-Computer Interaction, Information Systems, and emerging technologies, with particular emphasis on trust, ethics, and user experience in AI interactions.

I lead research initiatives within a major AI project, collaborating with industry partners to study how software teams integrate AI tools into their workflows. My published work has explored trust in AI decision-making across ethical frameworks and the complex relationships between AI interactions and workplace productivity.

My research has been published at top-tier conferences including HICSS, with additional work under review at several international conferences and journals. Building on this foundation, I'm expanding into Extended Reality (XR) research, particularly interested in designing ethical AI agents for VR/AR contexts.

I hold a 4.4 GPA distinction in Electrical Engineering from LUT University and am currently pursuing a Master's in Software Engineering with focus on Digital Systems and Service Design.

Research

Human-AI Trust & Ethics

Extended Reality & AI

Workplace AI Psychology

Exploring the psychological impacts of AI integration in workplace environments, including counterintuitive findings about emotional productivity and the human factors of automated assistance.
